Abyssian

Abyssian is a first-person, action-adventure game inspired by the Metroid-vania genre. In Abyssian there is an equal emphasis on exploration, puzzle-solving, and alien combat. This game was developed by a team of students under the name Pigfrog Studios using the Unity Engine for the final year of the Game Development program I was enrolled in. Listed below are a couple of key components I contributed towards shaping the final product.

  • Programming Contact
    Organized and distributed programming tasks amongst team of programmers to maximize workflow, as well as organizing and editing the Technical Design Document.
  • Lead AI Programmer
    Created modular Finite State Machine to handle AI behavior. With this system in place, it allowed the AI to switch through states seamlessly through wrapped functions and made it feel natural to add as many states as required for different types of enemies.
  • Build Engineer
    Ensured product was up to quality standard and stable for release, alongside porting for multiple platforms(Windows/Mac). This involved multiple iterations and bug fixing systems throughout the project's lifetime to ensure a stable release.
  • Optimization Lead
    Inspected coding processes using Unity's profiler to pinpoint costly bottlenecks and reworked them to enhance performance.
  • Health/Armor Systems
    Alongside my colleague Vanessa Rakoczy we created the main Health and Armor systems. These systems used coroutine procedures from the Unity Engine to consistently and accurately keep track of time based logic, along with basic integer iteration.
